description: Contemporary Chinese Village Gazetteer Data (数字村庄 / Digital Village)
  is an open dataset developed by the University of Pittsburgh that extracts and presents
  structured data from selected Chinese village gazetteers. These village gazetteers
  record detailed statistical and descriptive information about individual villages
  from 1949 to the present, documenting the history and development of Chinese rural
  communities over seven decades. The dataset includes demographic data, economic
  information, social structures, cultural practices, and local governance records,
  providing researchers with quantitative and qualitative insights into village-level
  changes during the People's Republic period. This resource is particularly valuable
  for studying rural transformation, agricultural policy impacts, collectivization,
  economic reforms, and contemporary rural development in China. The structured dataset
  format faciliatates comparative and longitudinal research on Chinese village development.
