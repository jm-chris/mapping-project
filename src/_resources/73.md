description: Dunhuang Academy's Digital Library Cave (数字藏经洞 / Digital Cave 17) provides
  free access to over 60,000 high-resolution images of Dunhuang manuscripts and artworks
  from the famous Library Cave (Cave 17) discovered in 1900. The platform aggregates
  materials dispersed across global institutions including British Library (UK), Bibliothèque
  nationale de France (France), Institute of Oriental Manuscripts (Russia), National
  Library of China, and numerous other collections holding Dunhuang treasures. Cave
  17 originally contained approximately 50,000 manuscripts and printed documents spanning
  4th-11th centuries CE, representing Buddhism, Daoism, Confucianism, Manichaeism,
  and secular texts in Chinese, Tibetan, Sanskrit, Sogdian, Khotanese, and other languages.
  Advanced features include crowdsourcing for transcription and annotation, OCR technology
  for text recognition, AI-powered search and analysis, and tools for comparative
  study.
