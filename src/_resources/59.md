description: Chinese Text Project (CTP / ctext.org) is a comprehensive open-access
  digital library of pre-modern Chinese texts, with particular focus on pre-Qin (before
  221 BCE) and Han dynasty (206 BCE-220 CE) materials. Founded by Donald Sturgeon,
  the platform now contains tens of thousands of texts spanning from ancient classics
  to late imperial works, organized by traditional categories including Classics (經
  jing), History (史 shi), Masters/Philosophy (子 zi), and Collected Works (集 ji). The
  site provides accurate transcriptions with parallel translations where available,
  powerful full-text search across the entire corpus, concordance tools, statistical
  analysis, and cross-referencing between editions. Advanced features include automated
  textual analysis, markup tools for collaborative annotation, API access for computational
  research, and integration with other digital repositories.
