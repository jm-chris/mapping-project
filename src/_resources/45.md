description: Hedda Morrison Photographs of China collection at Harvard University
  contains over 6,000 photographs documenting China from 1933 to 1946. Hedda Morrison
  (1908-1991), a German-born photographer, lived and worked in Beijing during this
  transformative period in Chinese history, capturing daily life, architecture, landscapes,
  cultural practices, and social changes during the Republican era and early wartime
  period. The photographs cover urban and rural scenes, religious sites, cultural
  traditions, and everyday life across various regions of China.
